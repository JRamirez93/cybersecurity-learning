# Class 02 – Risk Management

These notes summarize the main concepts learned in **Class 02 – Risk Management** of Cybersecurity Introduction.  
They are presented in a beginner-friendly style with technical terms explained for clarity.

---

## 1. Assets, Threats, Vulnerabilities, and Risks

- **Asset** – Anything of value to an organization (hardware, software, data, people).  
- **Threat** – A potential cause of unwanted impact to assets.  
- **Vulnerability** – A weakness in a system that can be exploited by a threat.  
- **Risk** – The potential impact of a threat exploiting a vulnerability.

---

## 2. Risk Management

- Process to **identify, evaluate, and mitigate risks** to protect organizational assets.  
- Includes policies, procedures, and controls.

---

## 3. Risk Assessment

- **Evaluation of risks** for each asset to determine likelihood and potential impact.  
- Helps prioritize which risks require attention.  

**Methods:**
- **Qualitative assessment** – Uses descriptive scales (High, Medium, Low).  
- **Quantitative assessment** – Uses numeric values to estimate probability and impact.

---

## 4. Risk Response Categories

Typical strategies to address risks:

- **Mitigate** – Reduce the probability or impact of a risk.  
- **Accept** – Acknowledge the risk and monitor it.  
- **Transfer** – Shift the risk to another party (e.g., insurance).  
- **Avoid** – Eliminate the activity causing the risk.

---

## 5. Risk Management Process

1. Identify assets and classify them by importance and confidentiality.  
2. Identify potential threats and vulnerabilities.  
3. Analyze and evaluate risks.  
4. Implement appropriate risk responses.  
5. Monitor and review risks continuously.

---

## 6. Attack Surfaces

- The set of points where an attacker could attempt to enter or extract data from a system.  
- Minimizing attack surfaces reduces risk exposure.

---

## 7. Asset Classification

- Assign categories such as: **Hardware, Software, Confidential Data, Public Data, Human Resources**.  
- Use a **classification matrix** to assign importance and confidentiality scores to each asset.

---

## 8. Asset Lifecycle

- Assets go through **acquisition, use, storage, and disposal**.  
- Risk management should consider each stage.  
- Ensure proper **data retention policies** are in place.
