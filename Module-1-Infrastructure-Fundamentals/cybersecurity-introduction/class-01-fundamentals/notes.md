# Class 01 – Fundamentals of Cybersecurity

These notes summarize the main concepts learned in **Class 01 – Fundamentals** of Cybersecurity Introduction.  
The goal is to be accessible for beginners and recruiters, so technical terms are explained with their meaning.

---

## 1. Cybersecurity vs. Security

- **Security** – General protection of physical and digital assets.  
- **Cybersecurity** – Protection of computers, networks, and digital information against attacks or unauthorized access.

---

## 2. Areas of Information Security

Some main areas include:

- **Network Security** – Protecting networks from unauthorized access or attacks.  
- **Application Security** – Ensuring software works as intended and is free of vulnerabilities.  
- **Cloud Security** – Protecting data and services hosted in cloud environments.  
- **Identity and Access Management (IAM)** – Managing who can access what resources and under which conditions.  
- **Information Governance** – Policies, procedures, and rules to ensure security compliance and accountability.

---

## 3. Cybersecurity State 2024

- Cyber threats are increasing in sophistication and number.  
- Awareness and training remain key defenses.  
- Security certifications are highly valued in the industry.

---

## 4. CIA Triad

Three core principles of information security:

- **Confidentiality** – Only authorized individuals can access sensitive information.  
- **Integrity** – Information remains accurate and unaltered.  
- **Availability** – Information is accessible when needed.

---

## 5. Authentication and Authorization

- **Authentication** – Verifying identity (e.g., password, fingerprint, token).  
- **Authorization** – Determining what an authenticated user is allowed to do.  
- **Minimum Privileges** – Users should have only the access they strictly need.  
- **Non-repudiation** – Ensures actions cannot be denied or disputed later.

---

## 6. Password Guidelines

- Use strong and unique passwords for every account.  
- Avoid password reuse.  
- Consider password managers to store credentials securely.

---

## 7. Legal and Governance Concepts

- **Due Care & Due Diligence** – Act responsibly to minimize risks.  
- **Information Security Governance** – Framework to oversee policies, compliance, and risk management.  
- **No Repudiation** – Ensures actions are accountable and verifiable.  
- **Implicit Denial** – Access is denied unless explicitly granted.

---

## 8. Security Certifications

- Certifications demonstrate knowledge and skills.  
- Examples: **CompTIA Security+**, **Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP)**, **Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH)**.
