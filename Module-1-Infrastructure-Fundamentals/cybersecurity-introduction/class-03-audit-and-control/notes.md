# Class 03 – Audit and Control

These notes summarize the main concepts learned in **Class 03 – Audit and Control** of Cybersecurity Introduction.  
Technical terms are explained to keep it beginner-friendly.

---

## 1. Access Control

- **Physical Access Control** – Measures to restrict entry to physical locations (server rooms, offices).  
- **Logical Access Control** – Restrictions on digital resources (systems, files, applications).  

**Models of Control:**

- **Mandatory Access Control (MAC)** – Central authority determines access permissions.  
- **Discretionary Access Control (DAC)** – Resource owners control access.  
- **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)** – Access based on a user's role in the organization.  
- **Attribute-Based Access Control (ABAC)** – Access determined by attributes (user, resource, environment).

---

## 2. IT Auditing

- **Audit** – Independent evaluation of systems, controls, and processes.  
- **Objectives of auditing** – Ensure compliance, detect issues, improve security posture.  
- **Internal Audits** – Conducted by the organization itself.  
- **External Audits** – Conducted by third parties.  

**Audit Artifacts:**

- Audit evidence (logs, reports, configurations).  
- Documents and reports for compliance and verification.  

---

## 3. Audit Frameworks

- Structured approaches and standards used to guide audits (e.g., ISO 27001, COBIT).  
- Helps ensure audits are consistent, complete, and repeatable.
